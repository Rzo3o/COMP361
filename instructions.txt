Grade points are awarded proportionally. These are not all-or-nothing
points. If a section is worth 10 points, and the students completed
75% of that section properly, then they would get 7.5 points 
(as an example).

Progress Report 2 - Specification Document
- 50 points shared by all members of the group
- Team Lead submits to the myCourses assignment box
- Rubric:
-- 20 points for diagram, tabular, and chart Syntax
-- 10 points for adhering to the spec table of contents (completeness)
      (students did not leave sections out unnecessarily)
-- 20 points for a professional description of the project 
      (good plan, good language, looks professional)
-- Can deduct up to 20 points for BS

Progress Report 3 - Work Checkup
- Pass/Fail shared by all members of the group
- Make an appointment with the TA/Prof assigned to your group
- Nothing submitted to myCourses
- The entire team must be present
- Order of events:
-- Prof/TA will make critical comments about your project plan
-- Prof/TA will make critical comments about your specification doc
-- Discussion about the project
-- Scrum-like questions: How is the project going? Everyone okay?

Progress Report 4 - Design Document
- 100 points shared by all members of the group
- Team Lead submits to myCourses assignment box
- Rubric:
-- 40 points for diagram, tabular, and chart Syntax (correctness)
-- 20 points for adhering to the design table of contents (completeness)
      (students did not leave sections out unnecessarily)
-- 20 points for appropriate use of technology & design
-- 20 points for demonstrated good understanding of planning
-- Can deduct up to 20 points for BS
-- Look for plan quality, good language, and professionalism

Progress Report 5 - Interim Demo 1
- 25 points assigned to each individual team member
- Nothing is submitted to myCourses
- Must make an appointment with the Prof/TA assigned to the team
- The entire team must be present
- One team member has the project on their laptop to demo
- Prof/TA do not touch the laptop (unless the team wants that)
- Order of events:
-- Teammates take turns showing what they have done so far
-- Prof/TA asks questions about their work to assess competency
---- Explain how you did this.
---- If x happens, how would you handle it?
- Rubric:
-- 5 points, quantity of work accomplished comparable with others
-- 10 points, explained their portion well
-- 5 points, showed competency in navigating through the code and app
-- 5 points, answered the "what if" question well

Progress Report 6 - Failsafe Checkup
- Pass/Fail shared with all members of the group
- Optional! The team can skip this progress report
- Nothing to submit to myCourses
- Only a subset of the team needs to be at this meeting
- Make an appointment with the **PROF** only (not the TA)
- Order of events:
-- If someone in the team has an issue with the progress of the project
   or with a team member or the workflow, this is the time to talk to
   the professor.
-- With the professor, a plan B will be determined.

Progress Report 8 - Final Demonstration
- 100 points assigned to each individual team member
- The entire team must be present
- Make an appointment with the prof/TA assigned to your project
- There is nothing to submit to myCourses
- The program must run on one team member's computer
- The Prof/TA is not permitted to touch your computer or use the app
- The Prof/TA can only observe the execution of the application
- Order of events:
-- One team member gives a complete run-through of the app
-- Teammates take turns showing their portion of the completed app
   (which may include the source code or file system)
-- Prof/TA asks questions about their work to assess competency
---- Explain how you did this.
---- Explain the rationale of why it was done this way (or, assessor
     complains about the way it was done, asking for an explanation)
---- Theory question or if x happens, how would you handle it? This
     is to assess the student's depth of knowledge in their part.
- Rubric:
-- 10 points for their portion of the program running at all
-- 20 points for graceful running (handling errors, easy to use)
-- 20 points for look-and-feel or API contracts for no GUI
-- 20 points for implementing everything they promised
-- 10 points for good-looking source code (like, IPACS)
-- 20 points for demonstrating good design (like design patterns,
      contracts, modular design, etc.)

Progress Report 9 - Acceptance Test
- 100 points shared by all the members of the team
- Team Lead only submits the source code to the myCourses assignment box
- Make an appointment with the Prof/TA assigned to the project
- A min of one person from the team is required to be at this meeting
- The program must run on the computer of one team member
- Order of events:
-- The team member will launch the app
-- The team member will give their computer to the Prof/TA
-- Without any help or communication, the Prof/TA will run the app
-- The Prof/TA will stress test the app
-- Later, the Prof/TA will look at the source code uploaded to myCourses
- Rubric:
-- 20 points, verify the app contains everything promised
-- 20 points, verify the app runs
-- 20 points, verify the app did not crash
-- 20 points, verify the app is graceful and intuitive
-- 20 points, verify that 70% of the code was not generated/copied

Progress Report 10 - Scrum Meeting Journal
- 50 points awarded individually
- Each student submits their journal to the myCourses assignment box
- Rubric:
-- 10 points for what worked
-- 10 points for what did not work
-- 10 points for tension points and how they were resolved or not
-- 10 points for things you would do again
-- 10 points for things you would not do again
